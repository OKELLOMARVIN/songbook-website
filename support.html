Frequently asked questions

Getting Started
What is SongBook?

SongBook is a mobile app for organising lyrics and chords. You create songbooks — named collections of songs — and add lyrics along with chord annotations for guitar, ukulele, or any instrument you play. It's designed for church worship teams, bands, concert performers, campfire sing-alongs, karaoke nights, music camps, and anyone who performs or plays music.

Is SongBook free to use?

Yes — SongBook is free to download and use. Free accounts can create up to 3 songbooks. To unlock unlimited songbooks, unlimited songs, and all premium features, there's a one-time purchase of USD $2.99. You pay once and keep access forever with no subscription or renewal.

Is SongBook available on Android and iOS?

Yes, SongBook is available on both iOS and Android. The core experience is the same on both platforms, though some platform-specific features (like the file picker) may behave slightly differently between operating systems.

How do I create a songbook?

From the home screen, tap the + button and give your songbook a name — for example "Sunday Worship Set", "Campfire Classics", or "Band Rehearsal". You can then add songs by typing lyrics manually, importing from a file, pasting from clipboard, or fetching lyrics automatically by song title and artist.

Sharing & Collaboration
How does sharing work?

When you share a songbook, SongBook generates a 6-character code (e.g. ABC123). Share that code with your bandmates, worship team, or anyone else you want to collaborate with. They enter the code in the app and instantly join the songbook.

You can set permissions when sharing:

  • View only — others can see songs and chords but cannot make changes.
  • Edit — others can add songs, edit lyrics, and update chords in real time.
Can multiple people edit a songbook at the same time?

Yes. SongBook supports real-time collaboration. When multiple users with edit access open the same shared songbook, changes sync instantly for everyone. This is ideal for bands doing live setlist management or worship teams building a Sunday set together from different devices.

How many people can I share a songbook with?

There's no hard limit on collaborators. Share the code with as many people as you like — the whole band, an entire worship team, everyone at a music camp. Each person just needs the SongBook app installed to join.

Lyrics & Import
Can I import lyrics from another app or document?

Yes — SongBook offers three ways to get lyrics into a song:

  • File import: Open a PDF or plain text file from your device. SongBook extracts the text automatically.
  • Paste from clipboard: Copy lyrics from Notes, a browser, or any other app, then tap "Paste" inside SongBook.
  • Fetch lyrics: Type the song title and artist and SongBook will search for lyrics automatically using a public lyrics database.
What file types can I import?

SongBook supports PDF files and plain text (.txt) files. For PDFs, text is extracted automatically. If a PDF is a scanned image rather than real text, extraction may be incomplete — in that case, using clipboard paste will work better.

Is it legal to store song lyrics in SongBook?

SongBook is a personal organisation tool. Many commercially released song lyrics are protected by copyright, and you are responsible for ensuring your use complies with applicable copyright law. SongBook is designed for managing your own original content or lyrics you have the right to use. Private songbooks are stored only on your device and never uploaded to our servers.

Chord Editor
How do I add chords to a song?

Open a song and tap the Chords button (🎸) in the top bar to enter chord editing mode. Each line of lyrics will show a row above it where you can place chords. Tap + chord on any line to choose from a list of common guitar and ukulele chords, or type a custom chord name. Long-press a chord pill to remove it.

What instruments does the chord editor support?

The chord editor is instrument-agnostic — it stores chord names (e.g. G, Cadd9, Am7, F#m) positioned above lyric lines, so it works for guitar, ukulele, banjo, mandolin, piano, or any other chord-based instrument. You can also set a capo position and a song key on each song for quick reference.

Can I annotate capo, key, and tempo?

Yes. When editing a song you can set the Key (e.g. G, Bb, C#m), a Capo fret number, and a Tempo in BPM. These display alongside the song title so everyone in the band always has the right settings at a glance during a rehearsal or performance.

Can I add section labels like Verse, Chorus, and Bridge?

Yes. You can organise songs into labelled sections such as Intro, Verse, Pre-Chorus, Chorus, Bridge, Outro, and more. This makes it much easier to navigate during a live performance, especially for longer worship songs or medleys.

Privacy & Data
Where is my data stored?

Private songbooks are stored only on your device and never sent to our servers. They are removed when you uninstall the app.

Shared songbooks are stored on our servers so collaborators can access them in real time. They are deleted when you remove the songbook or close your account.
